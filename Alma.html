Alma The Younger

Family: Son of Alma the Elder

Kids: Shiblon, Corianton, and Helaman[2]

Titles First Chief Judge and Prophet in the Nephite Nation[1]

Synopsis/Context

Alma the younger is the son of Alma the elder, who was a Nephite prophet who organized the church during the reign of King Noah [1]. In his early life, Alma the younger was wicked, oftentimes rebelling against his father and seeking to destroy the church, along with King Mosiah's sons. However, after a powerful, yet, life-changing conversion experience, Alma the younger converted to the very church he sought to destroy - eventually becoming the first chief judge of the Nephites and prophet of the church [2].

Throughout the book of Alma in the book of Mormon contains stories from Alma the younger's missionary experiences, including the Sermon on the Good Shepherd and his missionary work in the city of Ammonihah, his conversion story, and his counsels to his sons - all of which teach and/or reveal important aspects and doctrines of the gospel [1]

Life Experiences

Alma The Younger's Conversion

In Mosiah chapter 27, it describes Alma the younger(earlier in his life) as being a wicked and idolatrous man, despite his father being the complete opposite. Alma, along with the sons of Mosiah, sought to destroy the church. They were, unfortunately, successful in leading many believers away. One day, an angel appeared before Alma and the sons of Mosiah and spoke to them with a loud and thunderous voice, warning them of destruction if they continued to seek the church's destruction. Following that warning, Alma the younger collapsed and became dumb unable to move his limbs or talk for three days. In verse 11 of chapter 36, Alma describes his encounter with the Angel and their warning as a feeling of “great fear and amazement”. His father, Alma the elder, was rejoiced to hear of this spiritual encounter, and even invited many to witness what God had done for his son.

They began to pray and fast for the swift recovery of Alma the younger, and after two days and two nights, he was normal. Alma the younger had repented during the duration in which he was dumb, and from that point onward, began teaching the people, trying to fix the damages they've caused.

The conversion story of alma the younger is two fold: it shows the impossible action of trying to destroy or hinder the Lord's work, as well as showcasing the power of the atonement in being able to allow for the change of heart - no matter how wicked or how far off someone is from the straight and narrow path.

"Conversion of Alma the Younger[5]"

Nehor

Shortly before Alma the Elder and King Mosiah died, Alma the younger was appointed to be the first chief judge as well as high priest. However it wasn't long until a man named Nehor was brought before Alma to be judged. Nehor preached false doctrine among Alma's people, whom he became quite popular with - even establishing his own church based on his false doctrines. Nehor was soon confronted by a man named Gideon, who, as a member of the church, reprimanded Nehor for his false teachings. As a result, Nehor smites Gideon with a sword, killing him in the process. It was for this very action that Nehor was brought before Alma the younger.

Alma sentences Nehor to death for Nehor's priestcraft and for Gideon's murder. However, priestcraft and false doctrines continued to spread, particularly by those who believed in them. These spreaders of false doctrine began to persecute members of the church, and were able to get away with it because of loopholes in the law. Many member's hearts were harden and many fell away from the church. Those who stayed faithful to the church continued to press forward, despite being persecuted. They were also blessed with abundance, and because of their faith and generosity, they used those blessings to bless everyone in need, despite their personal circumstances.

As mentioned in Alma 1:12 “this is the first time that priestcraft has been introduced among these people”. While the span of Alma chapter 1 spans around 5 years, it's easy to see the dangers and how quickly Alma's people deteriorated into false teachings and doctrines. However, as explained in the latter half of alma chapter 1, those who were steadfast in the church were blessed, and that they used those blessings to bless others, whether or not they were a member of the church. This story highlights the importance and blessings that come with continued faith and endurance, especially in a time of precedence and uncertainty as Alma's people are experiencing in this chapter.
