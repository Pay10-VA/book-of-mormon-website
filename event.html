Christ Visits the Americas

Synopsis/Context

Preceding Christ's visit to the Nephites in the Americas, the Nephite civilization was quite prosperous and industrious. Unfortunately, they had also become very prideful, dishonest, and immoral [2]. Prior to the Savior's visitation, the Nephits experienced great storms and earthquakes, and darkness that lasted for three days, as was prophesied years prior [1]. Just how it was prophesied, the Savior visited the Americas where we taught the Nephites, ministered, and performed miracles for them.

Jesus Visits the Nephites

3 Nephi chapter 11 describes Christ's visitation to the Nephites in the Americas. It starts off with explaining the Nephites' experience with hearing God's voice - where it describes it as being neither harsh nor loud, yet, still causing piercing their cores and their hearts to burn. God testifies of the Savior, Jesus Christ, whom after, descends down to the earth from the Heavens. It must have been such a marvelous experience, so marvelous that it made every single person present quiet and grabbed their full attention. Because the Savior was dressed in white, the Nephites assumed He was an angel. To prove to the Nephites that He really was the Savior, Jesus invited them to feel His side, as well as the injuries on his hands - the physical marks made as a result of His crucifixion on the cross [1].

During this time, the Savior teaches the Nephites about baptism - encouraging all to be baptized in His name. He also gives Nephi and the 12 disciples the authority to baptize, as well as instructions on how to baptize (the same ones we use to perform baptisms today). Jesus instructed His 12 disciples to go about the Nephites to minister, serve, and to baptize. Among His many teachings, the Savior taught the beatitudes and gave a sermon similar to that of the Sermon on the Mount. Additionally, He teaches the Nephites how to pray - to do so in secret, to avoid vain repetitions, and to begin the prayer by addressing the Father in Heaven. [1]

After teaching the Nephites, He encourages them to go home to think and ponder about the things He'd taught them. Before doing so, Jesus healed those who were blind, deaf, leprous, lame, and dumb. He also blessed their children individually, and had angels descend from Heaven to minister to the Nephites. [1]
