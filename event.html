Synopsis/Context

Helaman is the eldest son of Alma the Younger, who was the first chief judge and prophet in the Nephite nation. In his youth, Helaman had learned of his father's conversion story and was always strong in his faith and in keeping the commandments. Helaman was also commanded, by his father, to take charge of his people's records alongside the plates of the Jaredites, which were previously in his father's possessions. Starting with chapter 45 in the Book of Alma, Helaman takes over the responsibility of writing on the plates, and begins to share the gospel with his people. It is towards the latter-end of the book of Alma do we see Helaman in action as he leads 2000 warriors in a successful military campaign against the Lamanites.

Military Leadership

Helaman and the 2000 Stripling Warriors

The Anti-Nephi-Lehies, or the people of Ammon, were a group of people who were converted to the church by the sons of Mosiah. They made an oath to never take up weapons of war again, and if they did, they would be faced with destruction. As a result, the Nephites took them under their wing and provided them with protection, in order for them to keep their oath. However, after seeing all that the Nephites have gone through to protect the people of Ammon, the people of Ammon began to want to break their oath in order to help their Nephi allies out. After having been convinced by Helaman to abide by their promise, they figured out that some 2000 of their own sons were qualified to fight - as they had not made any oath in laying down their weapons of war. These young men requested Helaman to be their leader, and as such, Helaman led them into battle.

The 2000 Stripling Warriors [3]

In a letter to Captain Moroni, Helaman explains that he and his army were favored by the Lord - which was why they were preserved. In one particular battle with whom they fought the Lamanites along with Antipus, the Nephites were able to force the Lamanite army to surrender, without any of the 2000 warriors dying. Throughout the span of the next couple of years, Helaman had led his 2000 warrior strong army through numerous battles, which were mostly highlighted in his epistles to Captain Moroni. His small army even increased by 60 this time. Despite their small size and lack of fighting/war experience, not one soldier from Helaman's 2060 warrior army perished, and it was all because of their faith and because of the miraculous power of God.
